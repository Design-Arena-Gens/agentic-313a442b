Proteus 8 Build Workflow

  1. Launch Proteus 8 and create a new schematic. Name it after the gate you are prototyping.
  2. Open the Pick Devices dialog and add TTL (74LS) or CMOS (4000 series) gate ICs: 74LS08 (AND), 74LS32 (OR), 74LS00 (NAND), 74LS02 (NOR), 74LS86 (XOR), 74LS04 (NOT).
  3. Place the chosen IC on the workspace. Use a dual-rail power supply (VCC = 5V for TTL, 3-15V for CMOS) and connect power (Pin 14) and ground (Pin 7) appropriately.
  4. Add SPST switches for inputs A and B. Connect them to VCC via pull-up resistors and to ground through the switch for clean transitions.
  5. Wire the switches to the gate inputs. Attach a logic probe, LED with resistor, or virtual oscilloscope to monitor the output pin.
  6. For composite circuits, cascade outputs into other gate inputs. Use labeling (net labels) to maintain readability.
  7. Enable the simulation, toggle switches, and compare the observed behavior with the live visualizer above to validate the design.
  8. Document the wiring with annotations and export the schematic or netlist for future reference.
